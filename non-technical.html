Dance Club depicts the name of not only one of the most happening clubs of VITCC, but also portrays the picture of a family brought together by their common passion towards dancing. We dance with *D*iscipline and *A*ttitude to bring something *N*ew and innovative and perform with *C*onfidence to *E*xpress our thoughts. There may be different teams representing different genres, but we always have and always will represent our dance club as one. We dance for laughter, we dance for madness, we dance for hope, we dance for screams, we are the dancers and we create the dreams!

The Debate Society of VIT Chennai is reputed all across the academic community for its excellence, it’s endeavors of success in myriad competitions and its immensely capable skills of organizing debating and oratorical events that yield maximum participant satisfaction. We, at Debate Society strive hard to make sure that every member of the club shall receive equal importance and opportunities so that they can develop their interpersonal skills, oratory skills and learn the art of diplomacy in the most professional manner possible.

VIT Chennai Campus’ Dramatics Club is vivacious, energetic, enthusiastic, excited, tough intellectuals and so much more. The Club’s spirit is quite symbolic of what drama means ‘I do’. The participants and actors who make up the Club are young aficionados of exemplary theatre and street drama. There has always been many a great performance on Campus and Off-Campus. The Club is focused on staging all plays discussing issues that are pure arcane to human rights issues to blending tragedy with comedy. The goal of the Club is to help students ‘find themselves’ and to carve a niche for an ecosystem that is highly competitive and resilient.

Designer Club intends to integrate inter-discipline minds to nurture ideas and engineer them into products. Club members are encouraged to brainstorm ideas using concept development groups (CDG) and the team identifies the skills required to engineer those concepts. Thus one of the major objectives would be to have training for dummies across the necessary engineering domains. Further there is always an expert lecture series on intellectual property rights and patenting part of the activities. This club is many times misconstrued with art & graphic design , which is not and is more of industrial design ! The various activities include CDGs conceiving ideas in structured brainstorming sessions, skill training sessions for dummies and also encourages to participate in inter-collegiate design contests. There had been interactions from industrial styling experts, concept designers etc. too in the past. Few developed concepts include innovative home appliances, medical instruments, table top holders etc. Interestingly computer engineering students have learnt CAD to mechanical simulation and mechanical students had sessions on embedded tools. This makes the club versatile for any disciple engineering student to nurture and bring their ideas to reality!
