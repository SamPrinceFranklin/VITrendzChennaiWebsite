We have all been through that confusing point in life, right? The time when you have to choose a new laptop! Sometimes, it feels there are just too many fish in the sea, or that all of them are too costly. Maybe you got a new PC but you still aren’t happy with your choice. Or maybe you haven’t thought of even getting one yet. Whatever your situation is, we are here to guide you through the process of deciding the best laptop for you needs. Vitrendz conducted an extensive survey regarding various aspects. We took these opinions and survey results and foraged the internet to find the best laptops according to VITians. And here is our conjecture…

1. HP 15s-FQ2071TU and HP 14s-DQ253TU

The HP 15s and 14s come with an 11th gen intel silicon and 8 gigs of ram. The 14s also has a variant that features Ryzen 5 It has 512 GB SSD and runs Windows 10. It comes pre-installed with MS office. One of most the impressive features of this PC is that it has 32GB of optane storage too. Intel optane is a multi-use storage device. This 32 GB can be used as RAM as well as ROM as an when necesarry. The i5 variant puts this device well in the most preferred 50 – 60k range while the i7 one might be heavier in the wallet.

2.HP Pavilion Gaming(2021)

There is no doubt that this PC made it to the top of the list, considering that it is a literal beast. Although the i7 chip and 16GB ram seems a bit overkill for a college laptop, many VITians went with the higher specs. It does have 512GB SSD, Optane memory and 4GB of Nvidia GTX 1650Ti. It falls in the 80-100k range and thus making it miss the 1st position. It’s a serious gaming laptop but its much more than that. If you are a fan of that power, this is the best fit for you.

3. ASUS VivoBook Ultra S15

This Asus Laptop managed to make it into the top 3 due a crazy spec sheet and a cute little feature. Starting off it has a 11th gen Intel i7 silicon, 8GB RAM as well as 512GB of M.2 NVMe PCIe 3.0 Storage. It has a 2GB Graphics card courtesy of Nvidia and still manages to be light at 1.8kg. The cute feature that was foreshadowed is that, it actually has a display on the track pad. Asus calls it a screen pad and we’d be lying if we said it didn’t look cool. All of this and it still manages to squeeze itself in the under – 85k range.

5. The ASUS Triplets (S14, K14, P4)

The thing about Asus is that, they know exactly how to make the optimal laptops. Not giving you too less and not giving you too much, seems to be their speciality. The Asus S14, K14 and the P4 are basically triplets born together with very minor differences. All three fall into the lovely 50-60k bracket, have an i5/Ryzen 5 processor, 8GB ram, 512GB SSD and have preloaded MS office. The S14 alone gets a 2GB MX250 dedicated graphics. All three of them are incredibly light at around 1.2 Kg. So u slip it into your backpack and you are barely gonna feel it.
